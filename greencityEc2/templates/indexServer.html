Machine Learning Platform for Urban Tree Canopy Monitoring & Green Equity
A Story of Data, Justice, and Leaves In many cities, access to trees is a luxury. Wealthier neighborhoods enjoy cool, green streets—while lower-income communities endure heat, pollution, and poor health outcomes.
Manual tree inspections are expensive and rare in underserved areas. But the need for data is urgent.

That's where GreenCity steps in
Like a digital guardian, GreenCity utilizes satellite vision and machine learning to identify where shade grows—and where it doesn't. It maps trees, analyzes canopy, and makes environmental inequity visible. With it, city planners, local advocates, and arborist groups can finally back up their instincts with real, block-by-block data.

Tree Canopy Isn't Equally Shared
Low income U.S. neighborhoods have 15-30% less tree cover. They're 1.5-4°C hotter
Tree canopy isn't equitably distributed in U.S. cities—low-income neighborhoods have 15-30% less tree cover, contributing to hotter and less healthy environments. Accurate, up-to-date tree canopy data is expensive and labor-intensive to collect, leaving many cities with outdated or incomplete inventories. Addressing this gap is essential for climate resilience, public health, and achieving tree equity.

Existing inventories are often:

  • Outdated (5-15 years old)
  • Incomplete
  • Costly ($1.80-$2.50 per tree)
  • Labor intensive
  • inaccessible for smaller cities

Data Science Approach
We used high-resolution aerial imagery (NAIP 0.6m/pixel, 10cm imagery) and city tree inventories (Pasadena Open Data). Our workflow combined computer vision and deep learning:

  • VGG-16 FCN: Detected tree locations via peak-finding on confidence maps, achieving 81% recall within 12m of ground truth.
  • DeepForest (RetinaNet/ResNet-50): Detected tree crowns, leveraging pre-trained weights and local fine-tuning. Multiple experimental runs with 60cm and 10cm imagery, plus targeted image enhancements, improved crown detection recall up to 89%.
  • Spatial analysis and model performance statistics were visualized in an interactive web application (Leaflet, Bootstrap, JS API).
